Rebuilding Credit After Bankruptcy in Chicago

Bankruptcy is not the end of your financial life -- it is a fresh start. Here is how Chicago residents can rebuild credit after discharge.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Strategies for Rebuilding

  1. Get a secured credit card -- Use it for small purchases and pay the balance in full each month.
  2. Check your credit reports -- Ensure all discharged debts are reported correctly. Dispute any errors.
  3. Pay all bills on time -- Payment history is the largest factor in your credit score.
  4. Keep balances low -- Aim to use less than 30% of your available credit.
  5. Avoid new debt -- Focus on living within your means.
  6. Consider a credit-builder loan -- Some banks and credit unions offer these specifically for rebuilding credit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does bankruptcy stay on my credit?

Chapter 7 stays on your credit report for 10 years from the filing date. Chapter 13 stays for 7 years. However, the impact diminishes over time and many people see significant improvement within 1-2 years.

Can I get a credit card after bankruptcy?

Yes. Secured credit cards are available almost immediately after discharge. Use them responsibly -- small purchases, paid in full each month -- to rebuild your credit score.

When can I buy a house after bankruptcy?

FHA loans may be available 2 years after Chapter 7 discharge or 1 year into a Chapter 13 plan. Conventional loans typically require a 4-year wait after Chapter 7.
